THE LIMIT

Let's talk about Infinity:


So first let's start with something we've all heard about before: Infinity.


Generally, Infinity is regarded as "the largest number."
We usually say how we've been to school an infinite number of times
... or even the time your brother stole an infinite number of candy from you.
But the catch is, it's not a number at all!




Let's look at an example:

How many numbers are there between 1 and 2?
Well there's 1.1
But also 1.11
But also 1.111
There are an infinite amount of numbers between 1 and 2!
In short, infinity is a "concept."



Let's look at this with a broad picture:

Here's a pizza. We all love pizza right?

We can all agree that 1/2 of the pizza is shaded in.



And here's 1/3 of a pizza. Pretty standard so far right?
So what happens... if we keep decreasing each slice?



Let's look at 1/80, pretty tiny huh?
This is very close to 0: In fact, its decimal value is 0.0125, about 1.25% of the pizza.



So now let's consider 1 over infinity. What would that look like? Would it even look like anything?
Remember, we just established that infinity is not a number. But what does that mean for our pizza slice?
We can't just say the pizza slice is gone because in theory, the pizza slice will be so small we can't even imagine it.
1 over infinity is infinitely close to 0, but it's not actually 0, because there are many other infinites standing between our 1 over infinity!
We say 1 over infinity, "goes to 0." It's infinitely small, but not small enough to be nothing yet.
